Invent a new voice from a description

Describe a voice that doesn't exist yet. Lumen designs it, you save it, and it's available across every audio tool.

Intermediate 5 min read Updated 2026-05-25

Voice Design lets you author a voice that doesn't exist yet. Describe what you want — Lumen synthesises a voice from scratch, plays you a preview line, and saves it to your library if you like it.

How to describe a voice

Three layers: person, style, texture. Examples:

A warm middle-aged American narrator with a touch of gravel, speaks at a measured pace with quiet authority.
A young French woman with bright energy, speaks quickly with light laughter underneath.

Gender, age, accent

These are seeds for the model. Pick the closest match — your prompt fine-tunes from there.

Pace & pitch

Default 0.5 is neutral. Drop pace for solemn, raise for energetic. Drop pitch for authority, raise for warmth (within reason).

Preview line

Always test with the actual content you'll use the voice for. A voice that sings audiobook narration may not work for ads. Change the preview line to something representative.

One-tap viral effects, used well

Melt, crush, explode, glow — when each of Lumen's effects works, when it doesn't, and the intensity that lands.

Beginner 3 min read Updated 2026-05-25

Lumen's effects are the same family Pika made famous. They look ridiculous in a great way and they're built for one-tap virality.

The catalog

  • Melt — your subject melts dramatically. Great for product reveals and reaction videos.
  • Crush — a heavy invisible weight smashes the subject flat. Pair with comedic timing.
  • Explode — controlled detonation. Use on objects you want to destroy “for fun.”
  • Inflate — subject puffs up like a balloon.
  • Levitate — gentle floating. The most cinematic of the bunch.
  • Paint — brushstrokes wash over the subject.
  • Cake-ify — slices through to reveal cake inside (yes, really).
  • Crystallize — turns to crystal in place.
  • Glow — radiates light. Subtle, hero-shot quality.

Intensity

0.6 is the safe default. Below 0.3 the effect is barely visible. Above 0.85 it starts breaking the source. Stay in the middle for finished work; push extremes only for jokes.
